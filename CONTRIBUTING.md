# Contributing to Vector Labs Studio

Thank you for your interest in contributing to Vector Labs Studio. This repository serves as our public operating handbook. We welcome contributions that help clarify, refine, and evolve our organizational principles and execution systems.

---

## 1. Purpose of Contributions

We treat our organization itself as an evolving system. The purpose of contributions is to compound the capability, clarity, and resilience of the Studio's operating handbook. We seek to capture enduring organizational thinking rather than temporary operational workflows or implementation practices.

---

## 2. Contribution Principles

To maintain the integrity of our knowledge base, contributions should preserve our core writing principles:

*   **Timelessness**: Focus on principles that remain valid across changing technical and organizational contexts, avoiding references to temporary tools, platforms, or configurations.
*   **Clear Boundaries**: Respect the defined scope of each document, avoiding bleeding adjacent concepts into unrelated files and keeping documents focused on answering their single fundamental question.
*   **Principle-Driven**: Provide guidance, cognitive frameworks, and reasoned judgment over rigid, step-by-step rules or procedures.
*   **Implementation Independence**: Keep handbook guidance agnostic of specific programming languages, frameworks, or runtime tools.
*   **Architectural Consistency**: Check that new proposals do not duplicate, redefine, or contradict existing handbook sections.

---

## 3. Pull Request Workflow

We use a lightweight, reviewable workflow to build confidence in every change:

1.  **Fork and Branch**: Fork the repository when needed and create a dedicated branch for your proposed changes from the latest `main` branch. Use a descriptive, lowercase branch name (e.g., `docs/refine-decision-making`).
2.  **Make Targeted Changes**: Modify the files directly relevant to your proposal. Keep pull requests focused on a single logical change to support effective review.
3.  **Submit a Pull Request**: Open a Pull Request targeting the `main` branch. Provide a concise summary of the changes, the motivation behind them, and a checklist of modified files.

---

## 4. Review and Commit Quality

*   **Review Philosophy**: We treat review not as a gatekeeping mechanism, but as a collaborative, confidence-building activity. Review is focused on the quality, clarity, and consistency of the work, rather than the seniority or background of the contributor.
*   **Commit Quality**: Commit messages should be clear and descriptive. We suggest conventional commit standards (e.g., `docs: refine decision evaluation criteria`) to maintain a clean repository history.
*   **Respectful Collaboration**: We maintain a culture where reasoning, evidence, and logical arguments are valued. All interactions should adhere to our [Code of Conduct](CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md).
