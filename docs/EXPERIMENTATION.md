## Purpose
This document defines the experimentation philosophy of Vector Labs Studio. It answers a single question: **How do we learn responsibly when certainty is impossible?** It establishes the principles we use to structure our learning, challenge assumptions, and gather evidence to support future decisions. Rather than detailing testing platforms, statistical methods, or engineering implementation details, these guidelines outline how we approach disciplined learning.

---

## 1. Learning and Confirmation
* **Learning over Confirmation**: The primary purpose of experimentation is to generate learning, not to confirm pre-existing beliefs or justify past choices. We value learning that updates our understanding over data that merely validates our current path.
* **Challenging Assumptions**: Experiments are structured to challenge our core assumptions and hypotheses. We design learning efforts to expose risks and uncover flaws in our thinking, rather than selecting parameters that guarantee success.
* **Evidence Over Belief**: We treat experimental results as valuable inputs that build our body of evidence over time. We let observed outcomes guide our future choices, even when they contradict our initial expectations.

---

## 2. Proportionality and Risk Management
* **Proportionate Design**: The scale and rigor of an experiment must be proportionate to the uncertainty, risk, scope, and significance of the decision it supports. We do not apply heavy testing processes to low-impact, easily reversible choices.
* **Preserving Reversibility**: We structure experiments to remain reversible whenever practical. This limits the blast radius of negative outcomes and preserves our strategic options, allowing us to pivot or roll back when evidence warrants it.
* **Value of Negative Outcomes**: Negative or unexpected outcomes are successful experiments when they yield reliable learning. They contribute critical evidence that prevents disproportionate investment in weak directions and helps us refine our hypotheses.

---

## 3. Decisions and Judgment
* **Accumulating Evidence**: We recognize that single experiments rarely provide complete answers. Evidence compounds over time through successive, varied investigations, gradually clarifying the path forward.
* **Decision-Supporting Role**: Experimentation is a tool to improve future decisions; it does not replace the responsibility of making them. Results do not automatically dictate path selection; they inform the judgment needed to evaluate trade-offs.
* **Complementing Judgment**: Experimentation complements, rather than replaces, domain knowledge and reasoned judgment. We combine observed evidence and constraints with strategic insight to make final product and technical decisions.

---

## Scope Boundaries
This document intentionally does not define:

- Specific A/B testing methodologies or rollout frameworks
- Feature flags, canary deployments, or technical release management
- Analytics tools, data schemas, or product metrics
- Statistical formulas, significance testing, or confidence interval levels
- Specific experimentation software or execution platforms

Where needed, implementation-specific guidance may be defined separately without changing these principles.
