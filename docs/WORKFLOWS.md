## Purpose
This document establishes the workflow philosophy and the common lifecycle of work across Vector Labs Studio. It defines how initiatives progress from initial discovery through execution to completion, regardless of the specific product, technology, or team. Rather than detailing task-management systems or software engineering processes, this document outlines how humans, AI agents, products, and shared knowledge interact to deliver value systematically.

---

## 1. Workflow Philosophy
We view work as the progressive refinement of clarity rather than the execution of isolated, static tasks. Work is a system-wide journey that compounds the capabilities of the Studio. We reject the separation of execution and learning; every action we take must serve to improve either the product, our shared knowledge, or the Studio itself.

---

## 2. The Stages of Work
We organize work into four sequential, yet cyclical, operational stages:
* **Discovery (Problem-First Validation)**: Work begins with an identified problem or opportunity. We do not commit resources to build solutions until the underlying user friction or capability gap is validated. The output of this stage is a clear, written statement of objectives and assumptions.
* **Specification (Proportional Planning)**: In this stage, we refine our understanding of the work. Objectives are translated into proportional plans, defining expected outcomes, constraints, and validation criteria. Planning rigor is scaled based on the risk, uncertainty, and reversibility of the work.
* **Execution (Collaborative Delivery)**: This is the active construction stage. Humans and AI agents collaborate in a unified workspace to build simple, robust systems. As work progresses and is exposed to real-world outcomes, assumptions are tested and clarity increases.
* **Consolidation (Closing the Loop)**: Once execution is complete, we capture and formalize lessons, extract reusable assets, and update our shared knowledge base. Work is not considered finished until this consolidation loop is complete.

---

## 3. Human-Agent Collaboration
Human contributors and AI agents interact at every stage of the workflow.
* **Unified Engagement**: AI agents automate routine actions, generate initial specifications, build components, and draft documentation, while humans provide qualitative direction, validate context, and review evidence.
* **Co-Evolution**: As agents execute workflows, their performance and constraints are monitored, feeding back into agent capability updates and workflow optimizations.

---

## 4. Built-in Quality and Validation
Quality is not an inspection phase added at the end of a project. It is built directly into every stage of the workflow.
* **Continuous Validation**: We validate our work continuously against expected outcomes and empirical evidence.
* **Continuous Review**: Review is not a gatekeeping event; it is an ongoing process of logical verification conducted by both human and AI peers. By keeping our foundations simple and our reviews frequent, we minimize rework and maintain sustainable velocity.

---

## 5. Knowledge Compounding and System Success
Every stage of the workflow is designed to generate reusable knowledge, ensuring that the entire organization grows more capable over time.
* **Stage-by-Stage Codification**: We document context, capture assumptions, and write down rationales as we go, rather than waiting until the end of a project.
* **Learning Feedback Loops**: Successes, failures, and bottlenecks encountered during the workflow are treated as feedback to refine the Studio's operating model, update agent instructions, and optimize shared templates.
* **Double Outcome**: The completion of work must achieve two objectives: it delivers immediate, sustainable value to the user (strengthening the product), and it leaves behind reusable assets and documented insights (strengthening the Studio).

## Scope Boundaries
This document intentionally does not define:

- Detailed testing, QA, or deployment procedures
- Architecture review board formats

These operational processes are documented separately and build upon these core workflow principles.
