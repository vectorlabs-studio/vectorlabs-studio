## Purpose
This document defines the philosophy of review within Vector Labs Studio. We treat review not as a gatekeeping or administrative approval process, but as a collaborative, knowledge-building, and confidence-building activity. This document establishes the principles of how work earns confidence before becoming part of the Studio's products, operations, or shared memory.

---

## 1. The Core Philosophy: Confidence Over Authority
Review exists to build collective confidence in a piece of work. It is not an exercise of personal or hierarchical authority.
* **Building Confidence**: The goal of review is to ensure that work meets the Studio's standards for maintainability, simplicity, and user value, elevating the collective baseline of the Studio.
* **Evidence-Based Evaluation**: Review is a process of gathering objective evidence and validating assumptions, rather than seeking arbitrary permission. Work is evaluated against outcomes, measurements, and logical correctness.
* **Refining the Artifact**: Review is focused entirely on the work, not the contributor. It is a constructive mechanism to refine systems and documentation, fostering a culture of mutual improvement and shared learning.

---

## 2. Proportionality and Continuous Engagement
Review is integrated into how we work, rather than acting as a final barrier to delivery.
* **Early and Continuous Review**: Review is not a single gatekeeping event at the end of execution. It occurs early—starting with specifications and assumptions—and continues iteratively as clarity increases.
* **Proportional Rigor**: The intensity and depth of review must be scaled proportionally based on the risk, uncertainty, size, and reversibility of the work. Routine or low-risk tasks require minimal overhead, while significant architectural changes, core operating principles, and irreversible decisions demand thorough evaluation.
* **Significant Artifacts**: Any artifact that forms part of the Studio's durable memory, core codebase, or operating frameworks deserves deliberate review to ensure long-term alignment.

---

## 3. Divergent Thinking and Shared Ownership
Friction and discussion during review are valuable mechanisms for refining quality.
* **Constructive Disagreement**: Healthy friction and divergent opinions are valuable. Disagreement improves outcomes when reasoning is made transparent, trade-offs are explicitly analyzed, and evidence is prioritized over subjective opinions.
* **Preserving Rationale**: The review process must capture and preserve the context, trade-offs, and reasoning behind major decisions. This ensures that future contributors (human or AI) can understand the historical context of a system.
* **Collective Ownership**: The act of review does not transfer responsibility or ownership of the work from the author to the reviewer. The author remains accountable, while the review serves to strengthen shared understanding across the Studio.

---

## Scope Boundaries
This document intentionally does not define:

- Specific code review procedures or interfaces
- Branching, merging, or repository permission structures
- Pull request formats or approval checklist templates
- Automated quality gates, linters, or CI/CD system integrations
- Specific collaboration, chat, or issue tracking tools

Where needed, implementation-specific guidance may be defined separately without changing these principles.
