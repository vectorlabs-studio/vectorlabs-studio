## Purpose
This document establishes the workflow philosophy and the common lifecycle of work across Vector Labs Studio. It defines how initiatives progress from discovery through execution to completion, regardless of the specific product, technology, or team. Rather than detailing task-management systems or software engineering processes, this document outlines how humans, AI agents, products, and shared knowledge interact to deliver value systematically.

---

## 1. Workflow Philosophy
We view work as the progressive refinement of clarity rather than the execution of isolated, static tasks. Work is a system-wide journey that compounds the capabilities of the Studio. We reject the separation of execution and learning; our actions should ultimately serve to improve either the product, our shared knowledge, or the Studio itself.

---

## 2. The Modes of Work
We organize work into four key workflow modes. These are not mandatory sequential stages; rather, they are recurring modes that may overlap, repeat, be revisited, or be applied with different levels of rigor depending on the work's size, risk, uncertainty, impact, and reversibility:
* **Discovery (Problem Validation)**: Work begins by identifying a problem or opportunity. Significant implementation should not begin until the problem is understood well enough for the investment being made. However, we encourage lightweight experiments, prototypes, and exploratory implementation when they are the most efficient way to reduce uncertainty.
* **Specification (Proportional Planning)**: In this mode, we align on goals and constraints. Objectives are translated into plans defining expected outcomes, constraints, and validation criteria. Planning rigor is scaled proportionally based on the risk, uncertainty, and reversibility of the work.
* **Execution (Collaborative Delivery)**: This is the active implementation mode. Humans and AI agents collaborate within a shared context and reviewable work to build simple, robust systems. As work progresses and is exposed to real outcomes, assumptions are tested and clarity increases.
* **Consolidation (Closing the Loop)**: In this mode, we capture lessons and extract reusable assets. The depth of consolidation and documentation is kept proportional to the risk, impact, learning value, and reuse potential of the work, avoiding unnecessary overhead for routine or low-impact changes.

---

## 3. Human-Agent Collaboration
Human contributors and AI agents interact at every stage of the workflow.
* **Unified Engagement**: AI agents automate routine actions, generate initial specifications, build components, and draft documentation, while humans provide qualitative direction, validate context, and review evidence.
* **Co-Evolution**: As agents execute workflows, their performance and constraints are monitored, feeding back into agent capability updates and workflow optimizations.

---

## 4. Built-in Quality and Validation
Quality is not an inspection phase added at the end of a project. It is built directly into every stage of the workflow.
* **Continuous Validation**: We validate our work continuously against expected outcomes and empirical evidence.
* **Continuous Review**: Review is not a gatekeeping event; it is an ongoing process of logical verification conducted by both human and AI peers. By keeping our foundations simple and our reviews frequent, we minimize rework and maintain sustainable velocity.

---

## 5. Knowledge Compounding and System Success
We focus our knowledge compounding on meaningful outcomes, ensuring that documentation and codification depth remain proportional to risk, impact, and reuse potential.
* **Proportional Codification**: We document context, capture assumptions, and write down rationales as we go, rather than waiting until the end, keeping the level of detail aligned with the complexity and long-term utility of the work.
* **Learning Feedback Loops**: Successes, failures, and bottlenecks encountered during the workflow are treated as feedback to refine the Studio's operating model, update agent instructions, and optimize shared templates.
* **Double Outcome**: Meaningful work should create value for a product or the Studio. When appropriate, we preserve relevant lessons, decisions, and reusable assets to improve future work, helping to strengthen both the product and the Studio's shared capabilities.

---

## Scope Boundaries
This document intentionally does not define:

- Software development lifecycles (SDLC)
- Specific project management frameworks (Scrum, Kanban, sprints)
- Task management or ticketing tool configurations (Jira, boards)
- Branching strategies or code integration workflows (Git flow, PR procedures)
- Specific release engineering and CI/CD pipelines
- Detailed testing, QA, or deployment procedures
- Architecture review board formats

These operational processes are documented separately and build upon these core workflow principles.
