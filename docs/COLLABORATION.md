## Purpose
This document defines the collaboration philosophy of Vector Labs Studio. It answers a single question: **How do contributors create outcomes together?** It establishes the principles of shared intent, collective capability, and mutual accountability that guide joint efforts. Rather than defining communication tools, meeting schedules, or project workflows, these principles govern how contributors integrate their work to build simple, robust systems.

---

## 1. Intent and Alignment
* **Shared Purpose**: Collaboration begins with a shared understanding of goals and constraints. Contributors align their efforts around the outcomes they seek to deliver, ensuring that individual actions serve a common mission.
* **Complementary Strengths**: We design collaboration to integrate diverse capabilities. Contributors (both humans and AI agents) combine their unique strengths, matching strategic judgment with automated scale to solve complex problems simply.
* **Cultivating Trust**: Effective collaboration is built on trust. Trust develops when contributors maintain transparency, respect boundaries, follow through on commitments, and remain open to feedback and new evidence.

---

## 2. Disagreement and Learning
* **Constructive Disagreement**: We treat healthy friction and divergent opinions as valuable tools for quality. Contributors challenge ideas objectively using logic and data, focusing on identifying the best path forward rather than winning debates.
* **Collective Learning**: Collaboration is a generator of shared capability. Working together enables contributors to exchange context, discover patterns, and accelerate learning, ensuring that individual insights benefit the wider system.

---

## 3. Coordination and Accountability
* **Proportional Coordination**: We coordinate work with the minimum overhead necessary to align efforts, manage dependencies, and reduce friction. The frequency and structure of coordination are scaled to the complexity, risk, and duration of the work.
* **Mutual Accountability**: Contributors hold one another accountable for the quality, integrity, and safety of their shared output. Accountability is collective; success is evaluated by the joint outcomes achieved rather than individual task completion.

---
